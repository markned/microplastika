Manifesto

Microplastika is an exploration of the human in the digital environment.

We live inside streams of information. Algorithms shape perception.

Identity becomes fragmented. The self is no longer whole.

It consists of layers, traces, and distortions.

Just as microplastic pollutes the oceans, Digital noise penetrates consciousness. Microplastika is the visual trace of this process.

The solvent dissolves form, just as the digital environment dissolves the boundaries of identity.

This is not destruction. It is transformation.

Microplastika is the generation of the contemporary human.

Jealousy originated from a previously completed painting that existed as a resolved and unified composition. Jealousy 2024 [original: canvas/oil, 60x50cm] Rather than distancing this emotional state from my practice, I allowed it to intervene directly in the material structure of the work. The canvas was repainted cut reassembled. This act transformed the surface from a continuous image into a fragmented and reconstructed object. Jealousy is not a representation of emotion, but the result of its direct impact on form. The visible seams mark the point where psychological experience and material reality intersect.
